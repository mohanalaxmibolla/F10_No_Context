Stage 4: The Small Intestine - Nutrient Absorption 🌊

The Small Intestine

Despite its name, your small intestine is actually very long - about 20 feet (6 meters)! It's called "small" because of its narrow diameter. This is where most nutrient absorption happens.

Villi: Tiny Absorption Fingers

The inside of your small intestine is lined with millions of tiny finger-like structures called villi. Each villus has even tinier structures called microvilli. This creates an enormous surface area for absorbing nutrients!

Compare: If the small intestine were flat, its surface would only be about the size of a piece of paper. With villi and microvilli, it has the surface area of a tennis court!

What Gets Absorbed?

Nutrients absorbed in the small intestine include:

  • Carbohydrates → Provide quick energy for your body
  • Proteins → Build and repair muscles and tissues
  • Fats → Store long-term energy and support brain function
  • Vitamins → Support various body functions
  • Minerals → Build bones and teeth, regulate body functions
  • Water → Keep your body hydrated

💡 Fun Facts About Your Small Intestine:

  • The small intestine has 10-40 villi per millimeter squared
  • Each villi has 1,000-1,600 microvilli
  • Total surface area of villi: about 250-400 square meters (the size of a tennis court!)
  • Food spends 3-5 hours in the small intestine
  • The small intestine receives help from the liver and pancreas (see the previous stage)

How Villi Absorb Nutrients 🔬

Villi are covered with a single layer of cells that allow nutrients to pass through into the blood. Inside each villus are capillaries (tiny blood vessels) that carry absorbed nutrients throughout your body. The nutrients are then transported to your cells where they're used for energy, growth, and repair!

Stage 5: The Large Intestine - Waste Removal 🚀

The Large Intestine (Colon)

The large intestine (also called the colon) is shorter but wider than the small intestine. It's about 5 feet (1.5 meters) long. This is where the final stage of digestion happens!

Water Absorption

The main job of the large intestine is to absorb water from the remaining food material (called chyme or waste). As water is absorbed, the material becomes more solid, forming stool (feces). This process takes about 8-12 hours, and the material moves slowly through the colon.

Other Functions

  • Bacteria: Your large intestine contains helpful bacteria that produce vitamins (especially B vitamins)
  • Mineral Absorption: Some minerals like sodium are also absorbed
  • Stool Formation: Water is removed, creating solid waste
  • Storage: Stool is stored in the rectum until you use the bathroom

The End of the Journey

Finally, the stool moves into the rectum and then exits your body through the anus during a bowel movement. The entire digestive process takes about 24-72 hours from the time you eat food to when it leaves your body!

💡 Fun Facts About Your Large Intestine:

  • Your large intestine is home to trillions of helpful bacteria (called the microbiome)
  • These bacteria make up about 1-2% of your body weight!
  • The large intestine absorbs about 1.5 liters of water per day
  • Average stool is 75% water and 25% solid material
  • Bacterial fermentation in the large intestine produces gas (which you pass)
  • Your large intestine is 6 times wider than your small intestine
