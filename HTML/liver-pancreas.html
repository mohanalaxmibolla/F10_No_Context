The Liver - Your Body's Chemical Factory 🫘

What is the Liver?

Your liver is the largest internal organ in your body (about the size of a football!). It has over 500 different functions, and many of them involve digestion.

The Liver's Role in Digestion

The liver produces bile, a yellow-green fluid that helps break down fats. When you eat fatty foods, bile is released and breaks fats into smaller droplets (a process called emulsification). This makes it easier for enzymes to digest fats.

Bile Storage: The Gallbladder

Bile is produced by the liver and stored in the gallbladder (a small pouch attached to the liver). When you eat, especially fatty foods, the gallbladder contracts and releases bile into the small intestine.

💡 Fun Facts About Your Liver:

  • Your liver produces about 1-2 liters of bile every day!
  • The liver removes toxins from your blood (detoxification)
  • The liver stores glucose (blood sugar) for energy
  • The liver makes proteins needed by your body
  • Bile is what gives poop its brown color (along with dead blood cells)
  • Your liver can regenerate if part of it is damaged!
  • The liver receives blood from both the stomach/intestines AND the heart

📖 Key Terms:

Bile: A fluid produced by the liver that breaks down fats
Gallbladder: Stores and releases bile when needed
Emulsification: Breaking fats into smaller droplets

The Pancreas - Your Enzyme Factory 🔴

What is the Pancreas?

Your pancreas is a long, thin organ that sits behind your stomach. It has two main jobs: one for digestion and one for regulating blood sugar.

The Pancreas' Role in Digestion

The pancreas produces pancreatic juice containing powerful enzymes that break down:

  • Carbohydrates → Pancreatic amylase breaks down starches
  • Proteins → Proteases break down proteins
  • Fats → Lipase breaks down fats

These enzymes are released into the small intestine where they continue breaking down food alongside the bile from the liver.

Regulating Blood Sugar

The pancreas also produces two important hormones:

  • Insulin: Helps your cells take in glucose (blood sugar) for energy
  • Glucagon: Raises blood sugar when it gets too low

These hormones are released into your bloodstream to control your blood sugar levels throughout the day.

💡 Fun Facts About Your Pancreas:

  • Your pancreas is about 6 inches (15 cm) long
  • The pancreas produces about 1-2 liters of pancreatic juice every day
  • Pancreatic enzymes are the most powerful digestive enzymes in your body
  • About 99% of the pancreas is involved in digestion; only 1% produces hormones
  • If the pancreas doesn't produce enough insulin, a person has diabetes
  • The pancreas is sometimes called the "workhorse" of digestion

📖 Key Terms:

Pancreatic Juice: Contains enzymes for breaking down all food types
Insulin: A hormone that helps cells use glucose
Glucagon: A hormone that raises blood sugar levels
Diabetes: A condition where blood sugar isn't controlled properly
