Stage 2: The Esophagus - Food Travels Down 🔻

What is the Esophagus?

The esophagus is a muscular tube about 10 inches (25 cm) long that connects your mouth to your stomach. It's like a food chute!

Peristalsis: The Wave Motion

Your chewed food (bolus) travels down the esophagus in about 5-8 seconds using a wave-like motion called peristalsis. The muscles in the esophagus walls squeeze in sequence, pushing the food downward - just like squeezing a tube of toothpaste from top to bottom!

Amazing fact: Peristalsis is so powerful that you could eat while standing on your head!

💡 Fun Facts:

  • The esophagus is completely smooth on the inside
  • A special valve (lower esophageal sphincter) prevents food from going back up
  • Peristalsis also happens in your stomach and intestines
  • No digestion happens in the esophagus - it just transports food

Stage 3: The Stomach - Breaking Down Food 🫙

What Does the Stomach Do?

Your stomach is a muscular, J-shaped organ that stores and breaks down food. It's much more than just a storage tank - it's a powerful mixing machine!

Mechanical Digestion: Churning

The stomach's muscular walls churn and squeeze, mixing the food into tiny pieces. This churning motion breaks down food physically into even smaller pieces.

Chemical Digestion: Gastric Juices

Your stomach also releases gastric juice containing:

  • Hydrochloric acid (HCl): Breaks down proteins and kills harmful bacteria
  • Pepsin: An enzyme that breaks down proteins into smaller pieces
  • Mucus: Protects the stomach lining from the acid

After 2-4 hours of churning and mixing, the food becomes a thick, soupy mixture called chyme.

💡 Fun Facts About Your Stomach:

  • Your stomach is about the size of your fist when empty, but can hold up to 4 liters of food!
  • The stomach produces a new lining every 3-5 days so it doesn't digest itself
  • Your stomach produces 1-3 liters of gastric juice daily
  • The stomach is very muscular - it can produce 20,000 contractions per day
  • Different foods stay in your stomach for different amounts of time

📖 Key Terms:

Chyme: The soupy mixture of food and gastric juices
Pepsin: An enzyme that breaks down proteins
Gastric Juice: The stomach's digestive secretion
